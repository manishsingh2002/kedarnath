Trek & Travel Plan

The Complete 8-Day Expedition

This itinerary assumes you fly into Dehradun or take the train to Haridwar on Day 1. The mountain roads are unpredictable, so buffer time is built in.

⛰️ 🛕 ⛰️
Day1
Surat → Haridwar / Rishikesh
Arrival at the Gateway
Arrive at Dehradun airport or Haridwar railway station. Transfer to a hotel in Rishikesh. This is your basecamp before the mountain journey begins.
🕉️
Evening
Triveni Ghat or Parmarth Niketan
Attend the mesmerizing evening Ganga Aarti. The spiritual energy sets the perfect tone for the tough journey ahead.
Alt: 1,030 ft
Day2
Rishikesh → Guptkashi (200km / 7-8 hrs)
The Mountain Road
Start at 6:00 AM. You will drive along the Mandakini river, passing through Devprayag and Rudraprayag. The roads are winding, and motion sickness is common.
🏞️
10:00 AM
Devprayag Sangam
Take a brief halt to see the dramatic confluence of the Bhagirathi and Alaknanda rivers. From this point onward, the river is officially called the Ganges.
⛺
4:00 PM
Check-in at Guptkashi / Phata
Rest for the evening. If you have booked a helicopter ticket, this is the area where the helipads are located (Phata/Sirsi). Get to bed early.
Eat a light dinner. Drink plenty of water to begin acclimatizing.
Alt: 4,327 ft
Day3
Guptkashi → Sonprayag → Gaurikund → Kedarnath
The Great Ascent
The most physically demanding day. A 16km steep uphill trek. You can walk, hire a pony/mule (₹3000 approx), or a doli/palki (₹8000 approx).
🚙
4:30 AM
Sonprayag to Gaurikund
Private vehicles stop at Sonprayag. You must take a shared government Bolero (₹50) for the last 5km to Gaurikund, the starting point of the trek.
Lines for the shared taxi start forming at 4 AM. Expect a 1-hour wait.
🚶
7:00 AM
The 16km Trek Begins
The path is paved but steep. Weather changes rapidly—sunshine can turn to freezing rain in 20 minutes. Stop at Rambara (halfway point) for Maggi and tea.
6-8 Hours
🛕
4:00 PM
Arrival at Kedarnath Base
Check into your GMVN tent or basic room. The temperature drops near zero at sunset. Witness the magnificent Kedarnath temple against the snow-clad peaks.
Alt: 11,755 ft
Day4
Kedarnath → Gaurikund → Guptkashi
Darshan & Descent
Morning darshan of the Jyotirlinga, followed by the long walk back down to civilization.
🙏
5:00 AM
Kedarnath Temple Darshan
Join the queue in freezing temperatures. Inside the ancient stone walls built by the Pandavas (and later Adi Shankaracharya), touch the sacred triangular lingam.
Don't forget to visit the Bhairavnath Temple (a short 1km hike uphill from the main temple) before descending.
🚶‍♂️
10:00 AM
Trek down to Gaurikund
Walking down is faster (4-5 hours) but puts immense pressure on your knees. Use a trekking pole. Shared taxi from Gaurikund back to Sonprayag, then drive to your hotel in Guptkashi.
4-5 Hours
Day5
Guptkashi → Rishikesh (200km / 7 hrs)
Return to the Foothills
A long drive back down the mountains. Rest your exhausted legs at a comfortable hotel in Rishikesh. Treat yourself to a good meal—you earned it.
Day6-8
Rishikesh / Dehradun → Surat
The Journey Home
Depending on your choice of Train or Flight, spend the next 1-2 days transiting back to Gujarat, carrying the blessings of Shiva with you.

Helicopter Booking Alert 🚁

Official helicopter bookings are ONLY done through the IRCTC Heliyatra website (heliyatra.irctc.co.in). Do NOT book through random agents online—scams are incredibly common. Fares range from ₹5,500 to ₹8,000 for a round trip from Phata/Sirsi/Guptkashi. They open about a month before the season starts and sell out in minutes.

Surviving the Heights

Altitude & Insider Tips

Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS)
At 11,755 feet, oxygen is 30% lower than in Surat. Headaches and nausea are common. Walk slowly. Ask your doctor about Diamox.
The Camphor Trick
Tie some Camphor (Kapur) and cloves in a small handkerchief. Sniff it while trekking to help clear nasal passages and ease breathing.
Trek Fuel (Food)
Rely on Maggi, Parathas, and hot lemon tea available at small shacks along the 16km route. Avoid heavy meals while climbing.
Network & Connectivity
  • BSNL and Jio work best at Kedarnath top. Airtel is patchy. Vi rarely works.
  • Cold drains batteries 3x faster. Keep your phone in an inner pocket close to your body heat.
  • UPI fails often at the top. Carry at least ₹5,000 in cash.
Physical Prep
  • Start walking 5km daily in Surat at least a month before the trip.
  • Do stair-climbing exercises; the trek is basically a continuous staircase.
  • If you hire a pony, you will still experience severe back/knee soreness. It is not an "easy" ride.
