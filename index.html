About the Journey

Why Kedarnath?

Kedarnath is one of India's most revered pilgrimage sites — home to one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, sitting at 3,583 metres in the Garhwal Himalayas. It's a journey of devotion, adventure, and breathtaking natural beauty.

🛕

Spiritual Significance

One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and among the 12 Jyotirlingas. The temple is believed to be over 1,200 years old, built by Adi Shankaracharya.

🏔️

Natural Beauty

Stunning Himalayan panoramas, the Mandakini River, Chorabari Lake (Gandhi Sarovar), and the majestic Kedarnath peak at 6,940m backdrop.

🧭

Adventure & Trek

A 16-km trek through alpine meadows, waterfalls, and rugged terrain. Options for pony rides, palanquins, and helicopter service available.

📅

Temple Open Season 2025

Kedarnath temple opens on Akshaya Tritiya (around late April / early May) and closes on Bhai Dooj after Diwali (around October/November). The exact 2025 dates are announced by the temple committee. Plan your trip accordingly — outside this window the temple is inaccessible.

Getting There

Route from Surat to Kedarnath

Surat doesn't have direct connectivity to the Kedarnath base camp. You'll need to combine train + road travel (or fly to Dehradun). Here are your best options.

Option A — Train + Road

Recommended

Most economical and practical for groups. Surat → Haridwar/Rishikesh by train, then road to Gaurikund.

Total journey: ~24–30 hrs
Cost estimate: ₹800–₹2,500/person (train)

Option B — Flight + Road

Faster

Surat/Ahmedabad → Dehradun (Jolly Grant Airport), then road to Gaurikund. Saves time but costs more.

Total journey: ~10–14 hrs
Cost estimate: ₹4,000–₹10,000+/person

Step-by-Step Route (Train Option)

1

Surat → Haridwar / Rishikesh

Take a direct train from Surat Railway Station. Key trains: Dehradun Express (19019) or Haridwar Express. Journey ~22–26 hrs. Book at least 45–60 days in advance during season.

Train · 22–26 hrs
2

Haridwar / Rishikesh → Rudraprayag

Board a shared taxi or private cab (NH7/NH58 route). Stop to see Devprayag confluence if time permits. Overnight stay recommended at Rudraprayag to acclimatize.

Taxi/Bus · 5–6 hrs · ~160 km
3

Rudraprayag → Sonprayag

Continue by road through Tilwara, Augustmuni, Ukhimath. Scenic but winding mountain roads. Sonprayag is the last private vehicle checkpoint.

Shared Taxi/Bus · 2–3 hrs · ~75 km
4

Sonprayag → Gaurikund

Shuttle service runs between Sonprayag and Gaurikund (7 km). Private vehicles are NOT allowed beyond Sonprayag. Government shuttle or shared jeep only.

Shuttle · 20–30 min · 7 km Stay here overnight
5

Gaurikund → Kedarnath Temple

The main trek starts here. Trek is 16–18 km one way with ~1,300m elevation gain. Alternatively, take a helicopter from Phata/Sirsi/Guptkashi helipads to Kedarnath.

Trek · 16 km · 6–8 hrs Helicopter option available
✈️

Flight Route (Faster Option)

Surat Airport → Dehradun Jolly Grant Airport (direct or via Mumbai/Delhi). Then taxi from Dehradun to Sonprayag (~230 km, 7–9 hrs). Book taxis in advance — demand is high during season.

Day by Day Plan

8-Day Complete Itinerary

Carefully planned to allow acclimatization, cover key sights, and complete the Kedarnath darshan comfortably as a group from Surat.

01
Surat → Board Train to Haridwar
Departure Day
  • Evening: Assemble at Surat Railway Station. Board overnight train to Haridwar (Dehradun Express or similar)
  • On train: Pack light snacks, water, entertainment. Train journey is ~22–24 hours
  • Tip: Book 2-tier AC (2AC) or 3-tier AC (3AC) for group comfort. Reserve window seats for mountain views closer to Haridwar
02
Arrive Haridwar → Visit Rishikesh
Haridwar/Rishikesh · Acclimatize
  • Morning arrival: Reach Haridwar. Check in to hotel near Har Ki Pauri
  • Afternoon: Attend Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri (evening aarti is magical — don't miss it!)
  • Explore: Mansa Devi Temple (ropeway or trek), Chandi Devi Temple
  • Day trip to Rishikesh: Laxman Jhula, Ram Jhula, Triveni Ghat, Beatles Ashram
  • Stay: Haridwar or Rishikesh. Good food options, last chance for Gujarati thali!
03
Rishikesh → Rudraprayag
~145 km · Mountain Entry
  • Early morning: Depart Rishikesh by 6–7 AM. Roads get congested after 9 AM
  • En route stop: Devprayag — confluence of Alaknanda & Bhagirathi forming the Ganga. Stunning viewpoint
  • Arrive Rudraprayag: Visit Rudraprayag confluence (Mandakini + Alaknanda). Explore the small town
  • Evening: Rudra temple visit. Good local dhabas serving hot meals
  • Stay: Rudraprayag — important for altitude adjustment (895m elevation)
04
Rudraprayag → Gaurikund
~75 km · Base Camp Arrival
  • Morning: Depart by 7 AM. Pass through Augustmuni, Ukhimath (Kedarnath's winter abode), Kund, Sitapur
  • Stop at Guptkashi: Ardhnareshwar Temple and Vishwanath Temple. Good lunch options here
  • Sonprayag: Register for the trek (biometric registration mandatory with Aadhaar). Pay entry fee (₹50/person). Park vehicles here
  • Shuttle to Gaurikund: 7 km by government shuttle (₹40/person). Gauri Kund — hot water spring, Gauri temple
  • Evening prep: Arrange ponies/palki if needed. Buy trekking gear/raincoat if not packed. Early rest — trek day tomorrow!
  • Stay: Gaurikund (guesthouses or pre-booked GMVN accommodation)
05
TREK DAY — Gaurikund to Kedarnath
16 km trek · 3,583m elevation · The Highlight!
  • 4:00 AM Wake up: Early start is essential. Crowd increases significantly after 7 AM
  • 5:00 AM Start trek: From Gaurikund (1,982m). Key waypoints — Jungle Chatti (4 km), Bheembali (6 km), Lincholi (9 km), Kedarnath Base (13 km), Temple (16 km)
  • Pony/Palki option: ₹2,500–₹4,500 one way. Book at Gaurikund official counters only
  • Helicopter option: From Phata (nearest) or Sirsi helipads — ₹3,500–₹5,000/person one way. Book weeks in advance online at irctc.co.in
  • Reach Kedarnath: By 12–1 PM ideally. Check in to pre-booked camp/lodge
  • Evening: Attend the magnificent Kedarnath Aarti (7:30–8:30 PM). This is unmissable — the energy is divine
  • Stay overnight: At Kedarnath — GMVN, private tents, or guesthouses
06
Kedarnath Darshan + Nearby Sights
Main Devotion Day
  • 4:00 AM — 5:00 AM: Abhishek Puja — special early morning ritual at the temple. Book in advance for VIP darshan
  • Morning Darshan: Queue management is strict. Token system usually in place — collect tokens the previous evening
  • 10 AM onwards: Trek to Bhairavnath Temple (1.5 km uphill from main temple). Outstanding 360° Himalayan views
  • Afternoon: Visit Gandhi Sarovar (Chorabari Lake) — 3 km from temple. Sacred lake where Gandhi's ashes were immersed
  • Evening: Second darshan, sit by the Mandakini river, soak in the spiritual atmosphere
  • Aarti again: Second time attending the evening aarti — each time is unique and emotional
  • Night: Rest. Temperatures drop to near freezing — extra blankets needed
07
Kedarnath → Descent → Guptkashi
Return Journey Begins
  • 5:00 AM: Final morning darshan and prasad
  • 7:00 AM: Begin descent to Gaurikund. Descent is faster but harder on knees — use trekking poles
  • Arrive Gaurikund by 1–2 PM. Take shuttle to Sonprayag, then taxi to Guptkashi
  • Guptkashi: Rest, hot meal, hot shower — heaven after the trek! Visit Ardhnareshwar temple
  • Stay: Guptkashi — good hotel options, comfortable beds!
08
Guptkashi → Haridwar → Train to Surat
Homeward Bound
  • Morning: Drive from Guptkashi to Haridwar (~190 km, 6–7 hrs)
  • Haridwar: Last Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri. Shopping — rudraksha, Uttarakhand herbs, trinkets
  • Evening/Night: Board return train to Surat. Arrive Surat on Day 9
  • Optional extension: Add Badrinath (Day 8–9) if time permits — only 100 km from Rudraprayag
Sacred Knowledge

Kedarnath Temple Information

श्री केदारनाथ ज्योतिर्लिंग

The Kedarnath temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to be over 1,200 years old. Built in North Indian Himalayan style with large stone slabs, it stands at the base of the Kedarnath peak (6,940m). The temple is revered as one of the 12 Jyotirlingas and one of the Panch Kedar. The lingam inside is a natural rock formation in a conical shape.

🕐

Temple Timings

Morning Aarti: 4:00 AM – 7:00 AM
Darshan Open: 7:00 AM – 3:00 PM
Afternoon break: 3:00 PM – 5:00 PM
Evening Darshan: 5:00 PM – 7:00 PM
Sandhya Aarti: 7:30 PM – 8:30 PM

🙏

Puja Options

Abhishek: ₹1,500–₹5,000 (early morning, book in advance)
Rudrabhishek: ₹3,000+ (special ritual)
VIP Darshan Pass: Available for priority queue
All bookings at official counters or via badrinath-kedarnath.com

📋

Rules & Etiquette

• Mobile phones NOT allowed inside the garbhagriha
• Remove footwear before entering
• Dress modestly — avoid shorts/sleeveless
• Photography restricted near the lingam
• Carry your own prasad or buy at temple shop

📝

Mandatory Registration

All pilgrims must complete biometric registration at Sonprayag registration centre (Aadhaar card mandatory). Registration is free but must be done before proceeding. Online pre-registration is also available at devasthanam.uk.gov.in — highly recommended to avoid queues.

Weather Warnings

Himalayan weather changes within minutes. Even in May–June, sudden snowstorms, hailstorms, or thunderstorms can occur. Always carry a waterproof raincoat. Check weather daily on IMD (India Meteorological Department) website. Never dismiss dark clouds forming over peaks — they signal incoming storms.

Our Recommendation for Surat Group

Best windows: May 15 – June 15 (opening season, maximum clarity) or September 15 – October 15 (post-monsoon, fewer crowds, brilliant views). Avoid July–August monsoon if possible.

Stay Safe

Safety Guide — Critical Information

⚠️

Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) — Most Important Risk

AMS affects many first-time Himalayan visitors. Symptoms: severe headache, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, confusion, shortness of breath. If symptoms appear, do NOT ascend further. Descend immediately if severe. Kedarnath is at 3,583m — your body needs time to adjust. Do not rush the ascent.

🏔️

Altitude Rules

• Ascend gradually — don't rush
• "Climb high, sleep low" principle
• Drink 3–4 litres of water daily
• Avoid alcohol and sleeping pills
• Rest at Rudraprayag to acclimatize
• Use pulse oximeter to monitor SpO2 (below 85% = danger)
• Diamox — take 24 hrs before ascent if doctor-approved

⛈️

Weather Safety

• Start trek before 6 AM — reach before afternoon storms
• Never trek in heavy rain or lightning
• If thunder begins, seek shelter immediately
• Check weather forecast daily (IMD app)
• Do NOT attempt Gandhi Sarovar lake trek if clouds are forming
• Keep raincoat accessible — not inside bag

🧭

Trek Safety

• Never trek alone — stay with group
• Inform guesthouse of your trek plan daily
• Don't leave the marked trail
• Carry whistle for emergencies
• Ponies have right-of-way — step aside
• Don't stop in narrow spots to avoid crowd stampede
• Carry headlamp for early morning starts

💧

Water & Food Safety

  • Only drink sealed bottled water or boiled water
  • Avoid raw salads, cut fruits from roadside stalls
  • Stick to hot cooked food (dal, rice, khichdi)
  • High altitude reduces appetite — eat even if not hungry
  • Carry ORS — altitude dehydration is common and dangerous
📱

Connectivity & Communication

  • BSNL network works best in mountains — consider a SIM
  • Airtel/Jio have patchy coverage above Gaurikund
  • No network at Kedarnath — download offline maps
  • Share your complete itinerary with family back in Surat
  • Satellite communicators (SPOT) available on rent for serious trekkers
🚨

People Who Should NOT Trek / Consult Doctor First

Those with heart conditions, severe asthma, high blood pressure, recent surgery, pregnancy, or any respiratory illness should consult a doctor before planning. People above 65 or below 5 years of age should consider helicopter-only visit. Never hide pre-existing medical conditions from your group or guide.

Insider Knowledge

Pro Tips from Experienced Pilgrims

🌅
Start the Trek at 4–5 AMAvoid the afternoon crowd, reach temple by late morning, and get the best mountain light for views. The trail is much quieter and safer in early morning.
📲
Download the Devasthanam AppThe official BKTC app has temple timings, weather, puja booking, and registration. Download before leaving — no internet above Gaurikund.
💳
Withdraw Cash at RudraprayagThis is your last reliable ATM point. Carry extra cash — digital payments barely work above Gaurikund and many dhabas are cash-only.
🎒
Leave Big Bags at GaurikundLeave your large luggage at your guesthouse in Gaurikund (paid locker service available). Carry only a 20L daypack for the trek to Kedarnath and back.
🌿
Respect the EnvironmentDo not litter — Kedarnath and its surroundings are fragile ecosystems. Carry your trash back down. Plastic bags are banned in the area. Use reusable water bottles.
📸
Best Photography SpotsBhairavnath Temple hilltop (360° panorama), Kedarnath Temple at dawn, Chorabari Lake reflection, Lincholi meadows, Mandakini River valley from above Bheembali.
🍽️
Gujarati-Friendly Food TipYou'll find plenty of vegetarian food all along the route. Stock up on dry snacks from Surat — chakli, mathri, energy bars. At higher altitudes, eat simple foods like khichdi, dal, and rice.
🧘
Pace Yourself, Don't RaceThis is not a competition. Trek slowly and steadily. Rapid ascent is the #1 cause of AMS. Take regular breaks at Jungle Chatti and Bheembali. The temple will wait.
🎫
Book Everything 45–60 Days in AdvanceDuring May–June peak, accommodation at Kedarnath and helicopter slots sell out within hours of going live. Set calendar reminders for booking opening dates.
🧴
Break In Your Boots Before the TrekWear new trekking boots on walks for 2–3 weeks before departure. Blisters from stiff new boots on a 16-km mountain trek are extremely painful.
